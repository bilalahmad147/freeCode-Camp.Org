Intro

It's really not a secret that sometimes people feel down, or anxious, or depressed, or angry, or [insert unfun thing here]. Having bad times and experiencing emotions we'd rather not is a part of being human. I find it useful to have a toolkit of things I can go look at to make things a bit better during those times. I am not a mental health professional and I don't want to steer you wrong. There are lots of places or helplines that offer advice if you need help. This is a list of a few things I find helpful.

Movement

Physical activity. I feel confident that there are studies that support this, but it's here because I've found it realiably works for me. A few of my favourite activities:

  • Dancing
  • Running
  • Walking
  • Yoga
  • Martial Arts

Music

I find music to be one the things that can most quickly affect my mood. Here are a few different types of music that I like to listen to depending on what I need in that moment. I keep playlists of these types of music on Youtube and one my phone for easy access when I want to listen.

Thankful

I find this one to be preventative. Listening to people sing about how thankful they are for what they have inspires me. I say pair with dancing like a fool for maximum impact

I find a lot of songs that inspire thankfulness in me in soca. I'm loving this song and this one and this one and this one right now.

Also really love this one in the non-soca sphere.

Sassy

When I really just need to say "No way. I'm rising above X!", it can help to channel a bit of rebellion.

Calming

This is especially helpful if I'm feeling anxious.

Silly

Sometimes you just need a laugh. For a funny story told in song, calypso's got some good ones.

Play

I think it's important to make things fun when possible. If public transit is overwhelming, I've found a phone game to be a good distraction. So many things, play a board game, sing and dance like a fool (This is really one of my favourite things! REALLY!). Find something that feels like play. Something just for fun.

People

When I feel down, I often don't want to see people, but sometimes texting someone, even just a smile helps me to turn a mood around.

If needed, reaching out for advice or just an ear from a helpline is also a good option.

Breathe

Take a breath.

Take a few breaths.

I find this can help to slow things down enough for me to calm down if I'm feeling stressed or, at least, remember that the situation might be worth looking at in a different way. I had fun creating some gifs based on one shared on FreeCodeCamp's blog and there are so many other helpful breathing coachers.

Everything

Combining

Sometimes thing don't work like you'd hoped. Combining a few nice things can help.


      if(music!= help){
        try(music + movement);
      }

Looking to the situation

The situation can also limit what can be done, but it's helpful to have a list of things that work for different situations.


      if (inPublic == true){
        try(breathe);
        try(playingPhone);
        try(peopleText);
      }

Learning

I think learning can come through trying random lovely things and with time focusing on what seems to work best in different situations. This:


    try(lovelyArray[random]);

Becomes something like:


    try(lovelyArray[2]);

Being OK
